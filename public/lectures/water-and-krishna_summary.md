# Bhagavatam Lecture: Water and Krishna

This recording is a spiritual discourse centered on the Srimad Bhagavatam, specifically discussing the fundamental principles of material nature and the importance of connecting all material experiences to Krishna.

### The Science of Material Elements
The discussion begins with a technical breakdown of the elements of taste and water as described in Vedic texts.
*   **The Manifold Nature of Taste:** Originally one, taste transforms into six varieties—sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, and astringent—based on contact with other substances.
*   **The Properties of Water:** Water is identified as the "cementing ingredient" of material elements. Its characteristics include moistening, coagulating, cooling, and sustaining life (*Apo maya prana*).
*   **Strategic Connection:** The speaker emphasizes that understanding these "building blocks" is not a distraction from spiritual practice but a method to see Krishna’s design in everything, from the saliva on the tongue to the construction of a house.

### The Psychology of the Conditioned Soul
A significant portion of the talk addresses the internal resistance and "anger" that individuals often feel toward the Divine.
*   **The Autocrat Concept:** Krishna is described as a "transcendental autocrat" (*Satya sankalpa*), meaning His will immediately becomes reality. This stands in contrast to the human struggle for control, where individuals often cannot even control their own minds or bodies.
*   **Envy and Anger:** The speaker posits that the root of material suffering is a deep-seated envy of Krishna’s supreme position. This manifests in relationships as "sense gratification"—friendships are often maintained only as long as the other person satisfies one's ego or senses.
*   **The Mind as a Chariot:** The mind (*mano ratha*) is described as an unreliable "hurricane" that constantly fluctuates between acceptance and rejection.

### The Practice of Sankirtan
The resolution to material entanglement is presented through collective spiritual activity.
*   **Spiritual Sustenance:** Just as the body requires water, the spiritual body requires *Sankirtan* (congregational chanting and glorification). The speaker notes that distributing transcendental knowledge provides "spiritual blood" for the dormant spiritual body.
*   **The Power of Association:** While individual practice (*japa*) is necessary, the "team" effort of a community (*Sankirtan yajna*) is more potent for purification and developing "sweetness" in devotion.

### Philosophical Nuance
A participant raised a point regarding Krishna’s unfulfilled desires: while Krishna is an autocrat, He desires for conditioned souls to love Him. However, because He respects their independence and free will, this desire remains unfulfilled as long as they choose to remain "rebellious." The speaker concluded by noting that a devotee's role is to make plans to preach and spread this knowledge, leaving the results to Krishna's will.
